# PRD: George Hotz vs Eliezer Yudkowsky Debate Website

## Overview
A comprehensive website that explains minute-by-minute the historic debate between George Hotz (geohot) and Eliezer Yudkowsky on AI safety, moderated by Dwarkesh Patel.

## Debate Context
- **Date**: 2023
- **Duration**: ~94 minutes
- **Platform**: Twitter Spaces (Live)
- **Moderator**: Dwarkesh Patel
- **Topic**: AI Safety, AGI risks, and the future of artificial intelligence

## Participants

### George Hotz (geohot)
- American security hacker, entrepreneur, software engineer
- Known for: iOS jailbreaks, PS3 reverse engineering
- Founder of comma.ai (self-driving cars)
- Creator of tinygrad (deep learning framework)
- **Position**: AI skeptic regarding doom scenarios; believes in slow, manageable AI progress

### Eliezer Yudkowsky
- AI researcher and writer
- Founder of Machine Intelligence Research Institute (MIRI)
- Author of "Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality"
- Creator of LessWrong rationality sequences
- **Position**: AI safety advocate; concerned about existential risk from superintelligent AI

## Key Debate Topics

### 1. FOOM Hypothesis (0:00 - 12:00)
- Definition of recursive self-improvement
- George's challenge to the "AI explosion" narrative
- Discussion of whether AI can "go critical"

### 2. Timing and Trajectories (12:00 - 25:00)
- When superintelligence might arrive
- Importance of timeline predictions
- Economic doubling times and growth

### 3. Intelligence Hierarchy (25:00 - 35:00)
- Suns, planets, and moons metaphor
- Human-AI cooperation dynamics
- The "chess engine" analogy

### 4. Goals and Instrumental Convergence (35:00 - 50:00)
- Orthogonality thesis
- Why AI might develop goals
- Resource acquisition drives

### 5. Technical Constraints (50:00 - 65:00)
- Diamond nanobots feasibility
- Search problem complexity
- Landauer limit and brain efficiency

### 6. AI Cooperation Problem (65:00 - 85:00)
- Prisoner's dilemma between AIs
- Whether superintelligent AIs would cooperate
- Game theory implications

### 7. Conclusions (85:00 - 94:00)
- George's optimistic view
- Eliezer's concerns
- Areas of agreement and disagreement
